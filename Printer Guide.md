# SUPERVISORY REFERENCE: PRINTER OPERATIONS

## SECTION 1: RAPID FABRICATION SYSTEM (PRINTER ARRAY)

Each PCE-C facility maintains a Type-III rapid fabrication array capable of producing licensed equipment up to LL1 specification along with basic day-to-day manufacturing. All print requests are authenticated against the operator's credentials and license level.

### 1.1 OPERATOR CREDENTIALS

Each supervisor is issued a credential chip encoded with a dummy pilot license (LL1).

Your credential chip should be stored in the supervisor's desk or on your person at all times. Lost chips must be reported to Site Administration within one duty cycle.

Credential chips are NOT to be provided to any non-authorized individuals, or to be integrated with the Facility's computer systems outside of the foundry.

### 1.2 WHAT THE PRINTER CAN DO

The fabrication array can produce any equipment, weapon, or system module available at License Level 1, including pilot gear and mech-scale components.

The system cannot fabricate items above LL1. Requests exceeding the credential's license level will be rejected. Do not attempt to override this restriction. The last person who tried is why we updated this section.

> **Attention!** Following repeated violations, future attempts to override this restriction will result in termination of employment.

### 1.3 RAW MATERIALS

The printer does not create something from nothing.

All fabrication requires feedstock: raw material cartridges stored in the supply room adjacent to the foundry. Check stock levels before queuing a print job.

The system will accept a request, consume partial feedstock, and then fail mid-print if reserves are insufficient. This wastes material and produces what Maintenance has taken to calling "expensive garbage."

In extreme cases, materials can be recycled into the system, provided they are sufficiently broken down.
