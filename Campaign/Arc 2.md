# The Reihenstadt War

## Inciting Incident

The gate goes down during the summit. Every faction immediately blames another. Within hours, the situation spirals into open conflict across the planet.

Von Coln's absence becomes critical without the planetary administrator, there's no neutral authority to de-escalate and most of Heimkrone sides with HA. If the players haven't found him yet, his fate gets answered inside a drone base during the war.

If the players have, then HA has snuck a large battleship into the system during the peace talks, which is landing an invasion force. They state that von Coln's admin is an illegitimate government

---

## Structure

The war consolidates into **three fronts**:

1. **Union vs. HA** (with Heimkrone falling to one side or the other — likely a Heimkrone civil war either way)
2. **Everyone vs. the Drones** (SecComm automated systems reclaiming territory)
3. **APHELION** (either a covert ally or an accelerant, depending on player choices)

**Player objective:** This is not a war to win militarily. The players are trying to _end_ the war through surgical strikes, intelligence work, and faction diplomacy.

Rough shape:

- **Phase 1** — Escape the summit. Fight to wherever Union is holding ground. They'll need to save the officals who are there too
- **Phase 2** — Establish footing. Surgical strikes to bleed the drone advance and shore up pro-Union Heimkrone elements.
- **Phase 3** — Find the kill switch. The drones can't be defeated in the field; the players have to trace the awakening back to the metavaults and address it at the source.

## Factions

### Union

The players' side, though not without internal friction.

- Most of OVERSIGHT (including the team)
- _The Last Refuge_ — marines, spaceborne assets, a handful of mechs, no other lancers
- Local peacekeeping forces
- **If Heimkrone doesn't ally with them:** Union seizes an old base on the planet's far side and digs in

### Heimkrone

The swing variable — their allegiance depends on summit outcomes. Likely outcome is a **civil war within Heimkrone**, with one faction aligning to Union and one to HA.

- Real standing army
- Significant local legitimacy

### Harrison Armory

- A flagship
- Elite merc lancer team
- Grunt soldiers (already present, secreted in before the summit)
- **Summit-dependent:** either aligned with pro-HA Heimkrone, or operating as a Heimkrone splinter

### APHELION

Split internally, which the players probably can't see clearly from the outside:

- **Non-HORUS elements** — trying to coordinate with metavault NHPs to free them; potentially a covert ally if players engage, especially through LSEL/Darj Tarats
- **HORUS-infiltrated elements** — same actions, opposite goal: manufacturing cascade events to harvest metavault technology
- Holds a few contested waypoints; primary influence is covert rather than territorial

### SecComm Drones

The third front. Attacking everyone without discrimination.

- Old nightside archives and automated systems waking up
- Reclaiming territory, constructing things — **building toward something**, not just defending
- **Root cause:** the drones are seizing the containment systems that were meant to hold the metavaults. Stopping them requires addressing MUNDLIFARI, not just fighting in the field.
- If APHELION coordinates with them, the front becomes significantly more dangerous and coherent

---

## Key Threads Carrying Forward

**MUNDLIFARI** — The arc's spine. The drones can't be stopped militarily. The players eventually have to trace the awakening to the metavaults and intervene at the source. Distributed failure points, not a single kill switch — multiple missions, not one climax.

**APHELION's split** — Players may need to work with non-HORUS APHELION to handle the drones, without being able to cleanly distinguish them from the HORUS wing. Darj Tarats has personal stakes here.

**Von Coln** — Resurfaces (dead or alive) inside a drone-held facility if not found before the summit.

**Charlie's identity** — Abelard makes the mistake _at the summit_, right before everything collapses. The revelation lands, then gets immediately buried by survival. The fallout carries unresolved into the war.
