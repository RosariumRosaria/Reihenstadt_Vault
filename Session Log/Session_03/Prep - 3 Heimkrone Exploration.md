# Heimkrone – Lowermost Ring (Promenade)

## 1. Arrival – Heimkrone Promenade (Lowermost Ring)

#### Visuals
- Spinning ring, gravity readjustment.
- Bustling promenade, orderly.
- Shops mixed: tourist + local.
- Large viewing deck:
  - Planet below.
  - Massive river.
  - Snow/ice hemisphere.
  - Desert hemisphere.
- Clean, intentional civic design.
Players can either poke around shops, or go straight to karte.


## General Atmosphere

- Clean, structured, civic architecture.
- Controlled lighting, neutral tones.

The promenade hums with:
- Vendors
- Families
- Workers in transit
- Light civic chatter

# Points of Interest

## 1. Civic Overlook (Viewing Platform)

A wide observation deck with reinforced transparent plating.

View:
- Massive planetary river splitting hemispheres.
- Snow/ice on one side.
- Desert and ochre terrain on the other.
- Blue equatorial band dotted with islands.
- Orbital elevator visible as a thin ascending filament.

Possible beats:
- Quiet character moment.
- Local explaining tidal lock.
- “Group windows” shown for viewing access times.

---

## 2. Market District

More crowded.
Color-coded signage subtly present.

Shops include:
- Precision metalwork
- Artisan textiles
- Practical winter gear
- Structured food stalls

Cultural notes:
- Vendors polite but reserved.
- Explain (Kronen, the local currency for non-essentials. Their comp/cons are loaded with a modest amount) 

Possible friction:
- Vendor checks their Grey status before serving.
- A “Green window” customer asks them what Grey is like.
- Mild comment about “Union efficiency.”


---

## 3. SecComm Heritage Exhibit

Located slightly off main promenade.

Displays:
- Old SecComm uniforms.
- Defense drones (deactivated).
- Historical footage of frontier stabilization.
- Framed quotes about order and civilization.

Subtle details:
- Language refers to “stewardship.”
- NHP doctrine referenced carefully.
- One display mentions “Containment Lattices.”

Possible seeds:
- A docent overly proud of SecComm logistics.
- Exhibit mentions underground defense installations.
- Some sections “under restoration.”
